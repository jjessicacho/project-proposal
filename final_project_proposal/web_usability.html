Competitive Sites

The following three delivery sites are all direct competitions with my site:

UberEats

  • Good:
    • Accurate heading image showing their best menu
    • Stand out buttons
    • Clean look with minimum color
  • Improvements:
    • No responsive order pages on mobile
    • No priorities on listing items as placing all columns horizontally
    • Too bright using only white backgrounds in header and body

DoorDash

  • Good:
    • Accurate Korean dish images in each items
    • Two vertical columns: menu item and shopping list
    • Two choices: delivery or pickup
    • Korean translations on menu title
    • Accurate typography separating headers and paragraphs
  • Improvements:
    • Too bright using only white backgrounds in header, body and even footer
    • No header image
    • Hidden address and store hours
    • Too narrow width of the secondary navigation
    • No hover animation when selecting items

SkipTheDishes

  • Good:
    • Well organized layout for using three vertical columns
    • Good choice of using light gray backgrounds in the body
    • Notification animation during closed business hours or out of location
  • Improvements:
    • Too long to scroll down
    • No images in any of items
    • Not accurate heading image

Note:

Most Korean restaurants in Vancouver have very poorly designed websites or even no websites at all. Therefore, I did not include them in this competition list.

My Favorite Site

Pizza Station

A local restaurant website that has great web usability and accessibility to promote their online ordering platform.

  • Why is this site useful?

    They aim for the same goal as my client wants while not relying on the 3rd party of delivery companies.

  • The main objective of the site: To promote their online pizza orders easily and quickly
  • Ways to deploy to achieve its goals:
    • Homepage and menu page all link to ordering directly
    • No 3rd party of delivery companies involved in their official website
    • Use high quality images in every single menu items
    • Sign up is not required in order to process the order quickly

Color Reference

Pies 'n' Thinghs

  • What kinds of the color value is used in this website? Light and warm colors using pastel yellow and red
  • Where and how to deploy their color values to my site? Following their color style, I can implement those pastel yellow colors as the main color of my site. Pastel red color can be my call to action buttons. Then, I can use brown as text colors.
